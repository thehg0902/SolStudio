# Objections & Closes

## Core Principle

As the reader approaches the CTA, excuses bubble up. **Never hide an objection — bring it onto the table and aikido it** into a pain point or a reason to act. Product-specific objections and universal objections both get addressed *before* they're voiced.

Diagnostic questions when planning any close:
- What reasons could they have for not buying?
- What little excuses could they generate to avoid acting?
- What hidden doubts might stop them from doing the right thing?

## The Common Objection Bank

**"I don't have time"** → Of course you don't — and there will never be a magic month when you do. If not now, when? The system exists precisely because you're busy.

**"I can't afford this"** → Reframe as a trust/value problem, not a money problem. (If a $200k car were on sale for $2k, the money would appear.) Raise certainty and shrink risk instead of dropping price.

**"I don't believe in myself / it won't work for me"** → Boat metaphor: the waves *behind* the boat don't decide where it's going. Past failures were missing the mechanism, not proof of personal defect.

**"I'm afraid of change"** → Acknowledge the unknown, shrink the first step, guarantee the floor.

## The Three Aikido Moves

### Move 1 — Acknowledge → Reframe → Target
1. **Acknowledge**: surface the objection yourself; soften and validate it ("You're probably thinking this won't work — that's a completely normal thought, I had it too")
2. **Reframe**: shift the parameters they're judging with ("the real question isn't X, it's Y")
3. **Target**: direct them to specific evidence that answers the *new* question ("as you look at these results, ask yourself…")

### Move 2 — Agree → Redefine → Target
1. **Agree**: yes, they're right — disarms resistance
2. **Redefine**: change what exactly you're agreeing to
3. **Target**: point at what settles the redefined question

### Move 3 — Exclusion
Qualify out the non-buyer. "If you're looking for [cheap/instant/no-effort], this isn't for you. We work with [serious identity marker]." Repels bad fits, magnetizes the identity your buyer wants to claim.

## CTA Rules

- **One path forward.** One button, one instruction. No forks, no mental tax.
- Make the instruction literal: "Click the link" / "Reply to this email" / "Call this number."
- Repeat the CTA through long copy — first ask early for the ready buyers, again after each proof block.
- **Ladder the ask to the trust level.** Don't propose marriage on the first date: cold traffic gets a micro-commitment (free assessment, quick reply); warm traffic gets the booking; hot traffic gets the sale. Escalate over time as trust compounds.

## The Boost (moving them from logical → emotional at the moment of action)

Most readers already know what to do — they need a kick over the hesitancy line. Stack 1–3 of:
- **Crank the pain or dream** — one quick movie in their head ("end it once and for all")
- **Scarcity/urgency** — limited spots, closing date (only if true)
- **Leverage prior commitment/identity** — "you said you wanted this"
- **Shorten perceived time-to-result** — "first calls booked by this afternoon"
- **Reduce perceived effort** — "we handle everything; you approve it"
- **Reduce risk** — guarantee, free revision window
- **Price anchoring** — give a comparison frame or they compare to $0
- **Reaffirm authority/trust** — credentials, results, local proof

## The Close Library

- **Simple direct** — just tell them what to do next
- **2–3 way close** — lay out their real options and let the others look bad: keep struggling / DIY at 10× the time / take the proven path
- **Success or excuses** — people are good at making money or making excuses, never both; pick one
- **Pain → relief cycle** — crank pain, relieve with the product; repeat 2–3 times
- **Hand-hold close** — narrate exactly what happens after they click (kills post-click anxiety: "you'll get a confirmation, then we'll ask 3 questions, then…")
- **"Are you serious about X?"** — I guarantee results; can you guarantee your side?
- **"Information isn't enough"** — if free videos worked, it would've worked by now
- **"This is what money is for"** — money sitting idle isn't safety, it's decay; this is the purpose it was earned for
- **Takeaway close** — "we're selective; we only take serious clients" → triggers "I'll prove I qualify"
- **Ghost CTA** — publish value with no ask; let inbound curiosity start the conversation

## Live-Conversation Aikido (calls, DMs)

- Pre-load answers: know the competitors' strengths/weaknesses cold so "why you over X?" has an instant, specific answer (trust, locality, availability, track record)
- Answer objections with calm certainty, never defensiveness
- If the objection is real and disqualifying — agree and walk away like a professional
