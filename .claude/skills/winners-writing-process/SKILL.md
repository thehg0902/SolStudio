# Winners Writing Process (WWP)

## Purpose

Never write copy blind. The WWP is the diagnostic run before any marketing asset is created. It forces clarity on who the reader is, where they are, what action we want, and what they must see/feel/experience to take it. Copy written without a completed WWP is guesswork.

## Position in the pipeline

1. Define the business objective (what needle moves, by how much)
2. **Run WWP** ← this skill (market research + top player analysis)
3. Write first draft (connect customer language to pain/desire)
4. Refine (self-review → outside review → client review)
5. Launch and measure

## The Core WWP Questions

Answer ALL of these in writing before drafting. Record raw findings first, structure second.

1. **Who am I talking to?**
2. **Where are they now?**
   - Physically: where in the funnel are they when they see this copy (scrolling feed, Google search results, inbox, GBP listing)?
   - Emotionally: painful current state, desires, frustrations
   - Market awareness level (1–4, below)
   - Market sophistication stage (1–5, below)
   - Current levels (score 1–10): **Desire | Certainty | Trust**
3. **What do I want them to do?** (One action. One objective per asset.)
4. **What do they need to see/feel/experience to take that action, given where they're starting?**
   - How will I get their attention?
   - How will I raise desire, certainty, and trust to threshold?
   - How will I lower perceived cost and thresholds?

## The Three Thresholds ("Will They Buy?")

A reader acts only when all three levels cross their threshold.

**Cost/Value threshold** — Is the value worth it?
- Levers: price, effort required, time to result, sacrifice

**Certainty threshold** — Do they believe the product works?
- Levers: guarantees, logic, science/credible sources, social proof, demonstration of result, how closely the product fits *their* situation

**Trust threshold** — Do they trust the company to deliver?
- Levers: familiarity and personal experience, social proof, vouching by people they trust, primal leadership indicators, certifications/qualifications

## Market Awareness Levels (pick one, it dictates the opening move)

| Level | State | The Move |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Problem Unaware | Catch attention → reveal the hidden problem/need |
| 2 | Problem Aware | Call out their problem → offer the solution |
| 3 | Solution Aware | Call out the known solution → position product as best vehicle |
| 4 | Product Aware | Urgency, scarcity, risk reversal, social proof, crank pain/desire |

Local service high-intent searchers ("roof repair near me") are typically **Level 3** — they know the solution category, they're choosing a provider.

## Market Sophistication Stages (pick one, it dictates the claim)

| Stage | Market State | The Play |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | New market | Make the direct claim |
| 2 | Second to market | Make a bigger, more dramatic claim |
| 3 | Tired of claims | Introduce a unique mechanism |
| 4 | Tired of mechanisms | Better/faster/easier mechanism |
| 5 | Tired of everything | Niche down, identity play, or experience play |

Most local trades/healthcare niches sit at **Stage 4–5**: don't out-claim, out-position (niche specificity, identity, trust experience).

## Market Research Template (fill completely)

### Demographics
- Gender, age range, occupation, income level, geographic location

### Painful Current State
- What are they afraid of?
- What are they angry about? Who are they angry at?
- Top daily frustrations?
- What are they embarrassed about?
- How does dealing with the problem make them feel about themselves?
- What do others in their world think of them because of this problem?
- If they described the problem to a friend over dinner, what exact words would they use?
- What is keeping them from solving it now?

### Desirable Dream State
- Magic wand: if life changed instantly, what does it look and feel like?
- Who do they want to impress?
- How would they feel about themselves in the dream state?
- What do they secretly desire most?
- If they described the dream to a friend over dinner, what exact words would they use?

### Values, Beliefs, Tribal Affiliations
- What do they currently believe is true about themselves and this problem?
- Who do they blame?
- Have they tried and failed before? Why do they think they failed?
- How do they evaluate whether a solution will work?
- What figures/brands in the space do they respect, and why?
- Character traits they value / despise?
- Market trends they're aware of, and their opinion of them?
- What tribes are they part of? How do they signal and gain status inside them?
- What can they accomplish *without* the product today?
- Why do the objections that killed previous purchases not apply to this one?

### Roadblock → Solution → Product chain
State it explicitly:
- Current state → Dream state
- **Roadblock**: the one key thing blocking them (roadblocks have layers — find the ONE key reason)
- **Solution**: "If they [solution], then they will [dream outcome]"
- **Product**: how the product makes the solution faster, safer, easier, more certain

Example — plumber: Current: leaky faucet → Dream: no leak → Roadblock: broken valve → Solution: replace faucet → Product: the plumbing service.

## Where to Mine Customer Language

Capture verbatim phrases — the copy must match the conversation already in their head.

- Client's existing customers and testimonials
- Competitors' customers and testimonials (Google Maps/Yelp reviews: read 5-star AND 1-star)
- Anyone personally known who matches the target market
- YouTube "my journey" videos + comment sections
- Reddit, Quora, niche forums
- Facebook groups, Instagram, X comments
- Amazon reviews on related books/products

### The reader's internal questions (answer them in this order in the copy)
1. What is this page?
2. How is this related to me?
3. Will it work for me?
4. How much effort/risk is this?

## Avatar Template

Build ONE person. Write to them, not to a demographic.
- Name, face (image), age
- Background and mini life history
- Day in the life
- Values; what they despise
- Outside forces/people influencing them

## Customer Buying Journey

Map the real path from stranger → paying customer:
- Would they actively search for this, or be interrupted by content/ads? (High-intent vs passive — see local-seo-gbp skill)
- Questions for existing customers: What were you looking for before you found us? How did you find us? Why us over competitors? What makes this unique to you?

## Top Player Analysis

- Pull winning ads from ad libraries (Meta Ad Library, Google search results for target keywords)
- For each top player: How do they grab attention? How do they establish authority? Where do they amplify pain/curiosity? What formula are they running?
- Model the winning structure; swap in the client's pieces. Model, don't clone.

## Niche Knowledge Vault (per new niche)

Answer before the sales call:
- How does [niche] make money? Average revenue and profit margin?
- Typical expenses, team size, operating model?
- Top players in the city — study their full online presence

## AI Leverage Prompts

- "Help me understand the buying journey for [product/service]. What steps do customers take, and how do I align marketing materials to each step?"
- "What questions should I ask [target audience] to understand their decision-making process for [product/service]?"
- "Identify the key pain points and objections [audience] has about buying [product/service], and how to address each in copy."
- "Guide me through the Winners Writing Process for my [funnel type] for [product/service]."
- Rule: AI accelerates structure and drafts. The human-to-human research (reading real reviews, real comments, real customer language) is never delegated.

## Definition of Done

- [ ] Business objective defined and measurable
- [ ] All 4 WWP questions answered in writing
- [ ] Awareness level + sophistication stage diagnosed
- [ ] Desire/Certainty/Trust scored with evidence
- [ ] MR template filled with verbatim customer language (not paraphrases)
- [ ] Avatar built
- [ ] Roadblock → Solution → Product chain stated
- [ ] Top player analysis complete with modeled structure
- [ ] Only now: draft copy (see copy-frameworks skill)
