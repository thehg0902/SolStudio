# Roofing — Target Audience Deep Study
> Studio Sol niche research | Ontario, Canada | For website copy, offer framing, conversion strategy
> Buyer studied: the roofing company's end customer (the person landing on the client's website)

---

## 1. Niche Snapshot

- **Core offers:** leak repair ($400–$2,000), full asphalt shingle replacement ($8,000–$18,000 GTA average), metal roofing ($25,000–$45,000), flat roof (commercial/additions), eavestrough/soffit add-ons
- **Sales cycle:** repair = same-week decision; replacement = 2–6 weeks, 2–3 quotes standard
- **Urgency profile:** dual-mode — panic (active leak, storm damage) and planned (aging roof, pre-sale, insurance letter)
- **Seasonality (Ontario):** peak May–October; ice-damming leaks Feb–March; windstorm surges spring/fall; winter = repair-only, replacement demand banks up for spring
- **Purchase frequency:** once every 20–25 years. Zero repeat purchase, 100% referral/review economy
- **Emotional register:** fear-dominant niche. This is not an aspiration purchase — nobody *wants* a roof, they want the problem gone

## 2. Target Market

### Primary persona — "Asset-Protector Dave & Sandra"
- Couple, 42–62, own a detached or semi in an Ontario suburb (Markham, Whitby, Barrie, Kitchener type)
- Household income $95k–$170k; home is 60–80% of net worth
- Roof is original or 18–25 years old — they've never bought a roof before
- Research pattern: wife often initiates (she noticed the ceiling stain / saw the neighbour's crew), husband compares quotes; joint sign-off
- Digitally competent but not expert — they Google, read reviews, check HomeStars, ask the neighbourhood Facebook group

### Secondary segments
- **Storm-claim Steve (35–65):** shingles on the lawn after a windstorm; insurance claim in play; wants photos, documentation, and someone who "deals with insurance"
- **Pre-sale seller (50–70):** realtor or home inspector flagged the roof; wants the cheapest legitimate fix that passes inspection, fast
- **Insurance-letter Linda (55–75):** insurer sent a renewal letter demanding proof roof is under 20 years old; deadline pressure, resentful spend
- **Landlord/property manager:** price-driven, wants invoicing and minimal tenant friction

### Ontario-specific context
- Freeze-thaw + ice damming = leak triggers that appear in late winter, convert in spring
- Insurers increasingly refuse/surcharge homes with 20+ year roofs → compliance-driven demand
- "Storm chaser" out-of-town crews after wind events have burned the market → trust deficit is high
- WSIB clearance + $2M–$5M liability insurance are expected proof points

## 3. Pain

### Practical pain
- Brown ring on the ceiling that grows after every rain
- Shingles/granules in the eavestroughs and on the lawn
- Attic mold smell; insulation soaked; energy bills creeping
- Can't physically inspect the problem themselves — total information asymmetry (they can't see their own roof)
- Quotes ranging $8k to $17k for "the same job" with no way to tell why
- Home sale conditional on roof; closing date pressure

### Emotional pain
- **Dread of hidden rot:** "What's it doing behind the drywall right now?" — every rainstorm is anxiety
- **Fear of being scammed:** deposit-and-vanish stories, storm chasers, "my brother-in-law got ripped off"
- **Financial ambush:** $12,000 they didn't budget for, arriving on a random Tuesday
- **Decision paralysis:** biggest trade purchase of their life, made once, with no expertise
- Shame of the visibly curling, moss-patched roof on the street

## 4. Desires

### Stated desires (what they say)
- "A fair price from someone reliable"
- "Fix the leak before it gets worse"
- "A roof that lasts and a warranty that means something"
- "Someone who shows up when they say they will"

### Deep desires (what they mean)
- **Never think about the roof again for 25 years** — buy peace, not shingles
- Protect the family's largest asset and the people sleeping under it
- Be told the truth by an expert — even if the truth is "you don't need a full replacement yet" (honesty here converts harder than any discount)
- A clean, drama-free process: magnetic nail sweep of the lawn (kids/dogs), no cigarette butts, done in 1–2 days
- Certainty: a written scope, a fixed price, a real warranty (workmanship + manufacturer), a company that will still exist in 10 years

## 5. Roadblocks

- **Price shock:** "$14,000?! For shingles?" — no mental reference point for roofing costs
- **Quote incomparability:** doesn't know what underlayment, ice-and-water shield coverage, ventilation, or flashing details mean — so quotes feel like random numbers
- **Scam wariness:** large deposits, door-knockers after storms, "we're doing your neighbour's roof and have leftover materials"
- **Contractor-vetting anxiety:** "How do I know they're insured? What if a worker falls on my property?" (WSIB/liability proof)
- **Disruption fear:** noise, debris, damaged landscaping, nails in tires
- **Timing friction:** good roofers booked 4–8 weeks out; panic buyers can't wait, planners get sticker-shocked into delay
- **Spousal alignment:** one wants the cheap quote, one wants the reviewed company
- **The deferral trap:** "It's only a small stain, maybe next year" — until the next storm

## 6. Perceived Outcome Solutions

*What they believe fixes the problem, and how they evaluate:*

- **Belief:** "Get 3 quotes and pick the middle one" — the default heuristic
- **Belief:** "Insurance should cover this" (often wrong for wear-and-tear; right for storm damage) — a company that clarifies this earns instant trust
- **Search behaviour:** Google "roofers near me," "roof repair [city]," "how much does a new roof cost Ontario," "emergency roof leak repair"; then Google reviews → HomeStars → company website → neighbourhood Facebook group
- **Evaluation criteria (in order):** review volume + recency → responds fast → shows up for a free inspection with photos/drone shots → explains options in plain language → insurance/WSIB proof → price
- **Decision trigger:** the first company that inspects, shows photo evidence of the actual problem, and gives a written fixed quote usually wins — even at a higher price. Speed-to-inspection is the sale
- **Misconceptions to correct on-site:** "new shingles over old is fine" / "all shingles are the same" / "a roof is a roof"

## 7. A Day in the Life

Saturday, 8:40 a.m., mid-March. Dave takes his coffee to the living room and stops — the faint brown ring on the ceiling is back, and bigger. Sandra saw it two weeks ago; he said it was "probably nothing, old stain." Last night's thaw says otherwise.

He climbs into the attic with a phone flashlight. Wet insulation. A dark streak down a rafter. He doesn't know what he's looking at, and that's the problem — he can't price this, can't scope it, can't even see the roof surface it's coming from. Twenty-three years this roof has been up there, and he's never thought about it once.

Back downstairs, Sandra already has three tabs open: a roofer with 480 Google reviews, a HomeStars thread, and a "cost of roof replacement Ontario" article that says a number that makes his stomach drop. They text a neighbour who repaved last year — wrong trade, but she knows "a roof guy." Dave fills out two website forms and calls a third company. The one that answers the phone, offers a free inspection Tuesday with photos, and says "we'll tell you honestly if it's a $600 repair or a full replacement" — that's the one that gets in the driveway first. Every rainstorm between now and Tuesday, he'll be staring at the ceiling.

## 8. Biggest Motivator

**Protection.** Of the single largest asset they own and the family living under it. This is a fear-relief purchase: they are buying the *end of anxiety* — the ability to hear rain and feel nothing. Hierarchy: (1) stop the damage/threat, (2) not get ripped off doing it, (3) never deal with this again for 25 years, (4) curb appeal is a distant bonus.

## 9. Website Conversion Implications (Studio Sol build notes)

- **Headline angles:** "Rain shouldn't be a stressful sound." / "Know exactly what's happening on your roof — free photo inspection." / Fear-relief > aspiration copy
- **Primary CTA:** *Book a Free Roof Inspection* (with photo/drone report promise). Secondary: emergency leak line, click-to-call, sticky on mobile
- **Trust stack (above the fold or near it):** years in business + local town name, Google review count/stars, WSIB + $5M liability badges, manufacturer certification (GAF/Owens Corning/IKO), written workmanship warranty
- **De-scam positioning:** "No deposit until materials are on your driveway" or clear deposit policy; "locally owned, not a storm-chasing crew" — say it explicitly
- **Content that converts:** before/after gallery by neighbourhood, "repair vs. replace — an honest guide," "what a roof costs in Ontario" transparency page (captures high-intent search), insurance-claim help page
- **Dual-path UX:** split hero — "I have a leak now" (emergency) vs. "I'm planning a replacement" (quote)
- **Proof of process:** photos of magnetic nail sweep, clean site, crew in branded gear — answers the disruption fear without words
- **Seasonal levers:** spring booking urgency ("May–June slots filling"), winter ice-dam repair page for off-season leads
