# Local SEO & Google Business Profile

## The Two Buying Modes (diagnose first)

**High-Intent Buying** — "Water is dripping through my ceiling" → searches "[town] plumber" → calls the best-looking result.
- Awareness Level 3: already sold on the solution, choosing a provider
- The most lucrative customer type: ready to buy now
- Win by: showing up when they search, standing out from competitors, removing friction

**Passive Buying** — not searching, but interruptible.
- Win by: Meta ads, organic social, referral loops (see funnel-playbooks)

Local service strategy = own high-intent first, layer passive second.

## Showing Up When They Search

Real buyers use a few predictable patterns:
- `[location] + [business type]` → "Houston plumber"
- `[problem] + [location]` → "fix leaky toilet Houston"
- `[service] near me`

Validate volume and find related terms with a keyword tool (Keyword Planner, Semrush). Two paths to visibility: **organic SEO** (compounding, slow) and **paid search** (instant, sits above organic — see funnel-playbooks).

**Timeline honesty for clients**: organic shows some movement in month 1, major ranking improvement takes 3–6 months. For money-in speed, run ads while SEO compounds.

## Google Business Profile (the local ranking core)

1. Claim and verify the listing
2. Accurate, complete info: business name, address, phone, website, categories, services, hours
3. High-quality photos and videos of real work — refresh regularly
4. **Reviews**: volume + velocity + responses. Respond to every review promptly, including negatives
5. Correct primary category; add all applicable secondary categories and pre-defined services

## Review Generation System

Post-service email/SMS, personalized per client. Working pattern:

> Subject: Regarding your last visit…
> "Thank you for choosing us, [name]. We're a small [category] and Google reviews genuinely help us. If you have one minute, we'd love to hear about your experience — the link below takes you straight to our review page. 👇 [link]"

Rules: one-click direct link, send within 24–48h of service, customize wording per client's voice. Never buy or fake reviews.

## On-Page Local SEO Checklist

- **Title tags**: primary keyword + location, ~60 chars, compelling
- **Meta descriptions**: unique per page, keyword + location, 150–160 chars
- **Headers**: logical H1/H2 structure; primary keyword in H1
- **Body**: keywords naturally placed; value-first, never stuffed
- **Localized landing pages**: one per service area / major service, each uniquely written
- **Local content**: posts about local events, projects in named neighborhoods
- **NAP**: name/address/phone identical everywhere, in footer sitewide, marked up with schema
- **Images**: descriptive filenames + alt text with keyword/location; compressed for speed
- **Internal linking**: guide users to service and contact pages with meaningful anchor text
- **Mobile-first**: fully responsive; test with Google's mobile-friendly tooling
- **Page speed**: a ranking factor and a conversion factor
- **Freshness**: regular content updates; monitor engagement to find pages needing refresh
- **Backlinks**: local news, community organizations, sponsorships, local influencers — participate locally to earn them

## SEO vs Paid — Client Recommendation Logic

- Client needs revenue this month → **ads first**, SEO in parallel
- Client has budget for one → ads for immediate ROI, unless their market has weak competition and their GBP is nearly ranking (then quick SEO wins may beat ad spend)
- Always frame SEO as the compounding asset and ads as the faucet — mature clients run both

## Tooling

Keyword Planner (volume/competition) · Semrush (keywords, competitors) · GMB Everywhere (GBP audits, competitor categories) · AnswerThePublic (question mining) · Seobility (site audits) · AdWords Wrapper (phrase/exact wrapping — never broad)
