# Sales Calls & Pricing

## Pre-Call Preparation

- **Niche knowledge vault** answered: how does [niche] make money, average revenue/margins, typical expenses, top local players and their online presence
- Their specific business audited: website, GBP, ads, reviews, obvious funnel gaps
- Hypothesis of their #1 growth bottleneck (to be verified, not assumed, on the call)
- Case study / relevant proof ready to screen-share
- Logistics: tech tested, quiet room, notes template open

## Frame

- They took the call for a reason — **they want something.** Remember it when nerves hit.
- Abundance: you have infinite options; this is one conversation of many
- Nonverbals: slow breathing, relaxed tone, active listening, don't rush, dress sharp, high energy
- You are the expert conducting a diagnosis — not a vendor auditioning

## Call Flow

1. **Rapport** — brief, human, genuine
2. **Reason for the call**: "I want to make sure what I show you is actually relevant to your situation — so tell me…"
3. **SPIN questions** (below)
4. **Verify the problem matches your solution.** If it doesn't: find their real problem and pitch to that, or walk away professionally. Never force a mismatched project.
5. **Present the strategy** — the exact approach that produced your previous result, mapped onto their situation
6. **Set the price via project math** (below)
7. **Close or schedule the decision** — never leave a call without a defined next step and date

## SPIN Question Bank

**Situation** — establish facts:
- What does the business look like today? (revenue, team size, offerings)
- Who is your target market?
- What are you currently doing to get clients?

**Problem** — surface pain:
- What challenges are you facing right now?
- What's stopping you from hitting your goals?

**Implication** — make the cost of inaction real:
- If this stays unsolved, what does the next 6–12 months look like?
- How does this affect your team, operations, family time?

**Need-Payoff** — let them sell themselves:
- If this were solved, what would the business look like?
- What specific results would make this a win for you? (numbers)

Between the discovery call and the pitch: **take a break before proposing the solution.** Diagnose on call one; prescribe on call two (or later the same call after a deliberate pause). Verify the offer matches their customers' buying journey.

## Qualification (walk-away rules)

- Can't fund the minimum ad budget or fee → not a client yet; leave the door open
- Decent business, moderately successful, has proven they can help people → good client profile
- If they don't qualify, exit gracefully — a bad-fit client costs more than no client

## Pricing: The Project Math

1. Estimate the revenue impact of the project (new clients × average job value × close rate)
2. Anchor your fee as a fraction of that value — the fee must be an obviously good trade
3. Structures:
   - **Performance**: 10–20% of generated revenue (best for first project — easy yes, aligned incentives)
   - **Flat**: $500–1,500/mo typical for local discovery-tier projects
   - **Hybrid**: setup fee + performance (target $1k+ upfront plus profit share once proven)
4. Walk them through the math out loud — the price should feel like a conclusion, not a demand

## Recap Email (send same day)

> Hi [Name],
> Thanks for taking the time earlier — I enjoyed learning about your goals.
> Recapping what we discussed:
> **Goal**: [specific, e.g., 20 new clients/month]
> **Proposed plan**: [milestone + date] → [milestone + date] → [result + date]
> If anything needs adjusting to fit your goals, tell me and we'll refine it.
> [Name]

Dated milestones create momentum and make you the only marketer who showed up with a schedule.

## The KCS (Killer Case Study) — Your Compounding Asset

Three pillars of a strong KCS:
1. **A powerful outcome** — big AND specific, tied to money-in (revenue, leads, bookings)
2. **A winning strategy** — logical cause-and-effect with a unique twist
3. **Deep industry insight** — you understand both the end customer and how the owner thinks

How prospects judge it: *Is this relevant to my business? How big and real is the outcome? Can I believe they'd do it for me?*

## Upselling Existing Clients

Until they're a $100M/year company, there is always a next project.

1. **Schedule a review & growth-planning meeting** after a win
2. **Run implication questions in reverse** — walk through all the *good* implications of the result just delivered
3. **Identify the next project**: raise volume on the winning strategy, or open the next funnel
4. **Value it** with implication + need-payoff questions
5. **Get agreement and payment** using project math
6. Any well-managed client is worth six figures over the relationship — protect it with over-delivery

## Testimonial Collection (60-second video, immediately after a win)

Three questions:
- **Before**: "What were you struggling with before we started working together?"
- **During**: "What did we implement?"
- **After**: "What results did you see, and what new opportunities does this create?"

## Next-Client Targeting (one level up)

- Stay in the niche of your KCS — the proof transfers at full strength
- Target businesses slightly bigger than the last client (they see your result and want the same)
- Target markets that can pay; strong local economies only
- Tools: LinkedIn Sales Navigator, Apollo/Crunchbase, Google + directories (or lead_scraper pipeline)
