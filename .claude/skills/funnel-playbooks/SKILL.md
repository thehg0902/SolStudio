# Funnel Playbooks

## Rule Zero

**Every funnel still requires full Market Research + WWP to generate results.** The funnel is distribution; the WWP is why it converts.

## Funnel Selection Matrix

| Funnel | Best for | Requirements | Speed |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Google Ads lead-gen** | Local services people actively search for (roofing, HVAC, plumbing, dental, chiro) — high-intent | Client ad budget ≥ $300–350 | Fast; highest intent |
| **Meta ads intro-offer** | Local businesses + visual services (med spa, gyms, salons, clinics) | $300–1,000 budget + a compelling intro offer | 48–72h to first leads |
| **Organic social DM** | Clients with ≥1k engaged followers and a proven product | Zero ad spend | Fast; ideal discovery project |

Don't take on ad-funnel projects for clients who can't fund minimum budget — the math can't work and it burns the case study.

## Playbook 1 — Google Ads Lead-Gen Funnel

**Why it works**: targets people searching with buying intent right now ("plumber near me"). A good ad sits above all organic results — instant top position.

### Steps
1. **Account setup**: client creates their own Google Ads account and owns billing; they add you as a user. Client pays for ad spend, always.
2. **Keyword research** (Keyword Planner → Discover New Keywords):
   - Think as the customer: `[service] near me`, `[service] in [city]`, `[problem] [city]`
   - Select for: high volume, **low–medium competition**, **high intent** ("book physiotherapy Toronto" beats "physiotherapy")
3. **Match types**: use **exact** and **phrase**. Avoid broad match — it drains budget on garbage queries.
4. **Campaign structure**: tight ad groups per service; ad copy mirrors the exact search intent; location targeting to service radius.
5. **Landing page match**: the page must answer the exact search that brought them (message match), with friction-free contact/booking.
6. **Measure**: cost per lead, booked-job rate. Kill losers, feed winners.

## Playbook 2 — Meta Ads Intro-Offer Funnel

**Why it works**: interrupts passive scrollers with a low-risk, high-value first transaction, then upsells.

### Steps
1. **Design the intro offer** — the single most important step:
   - Low-risk + high-value: free assessment + $20 service, bonus time added free, free trial + bonus session, bundled freebie
   - Time-box it ("this week only") for urgency
   - Draft 3–4 offer candidates; validate with people inside the target market before spending
2. **Create the ad**:
   - Copy: dead simple — headline hits pain/desire, 2–3 lines of body, one CTA
   - Visual: bold image or 15–30s video showing the service in action + offer text overlay; video generally outperforms
3. **Campaign setup** (Ads Manager):
   - Objective: Lead Generation or Messages
   - Budget: $10–30/day to start
   - Targeting: local radius, age/gender matched to avatar, 1–3 interest keywords
4. **Capture & convert**:
   - DM path: instant auto-reply → propose specific date/time → confirm with phone number
   - Landing path: one-pager with offer headline, countdown, single CTA
5. **Scale**: at 5–10 leads, double budget; test creative variants (image, headline, CTA); refresh creatives every few weeks in small markets
6. **Upsell**: intro offer → full-price package/membership (e.g., $20 assessment → $500+ plan)

FAQ notes: works with zero followers (ads target strangers); if client resists discounts, use added-value offers instead; 20k population target area is enough.

## Playbook 3 — Organic Social Media DM Funnel

**Why it works**: zero-cost discovery project that produces quick wins, trust, and a paid second project.

### Steps
1. **Intro offer** — same rules as Meta playbook; plan the upsell before launch
2. **Bold post media** — scroll-stopping image (client photo or generated)
3. **Body text** — 3 concrete reasons to act now + urgency + keyword CTA: "DM us the word **[WORD]**"
4. **DM conversation scripts** — pre-write the flows:
   - *Urgency close*: thank → propose time → "we're booking up, this offer ends [date]" → lock the slot → add-on question ("want us to handle any small issues we find while there?")
   - *Hesitation handling*: validate → cost-of-inaction ("small issues become expensive ones") → free-value reminder → propose specific day

## Pricing These Projects

- **Flat fee**: $500–1,500/month
- **Performance**: 10–20% of revenue generated — recommended for discovery/first projects (aligned incentives, easy yes)
- **Hybrid**: small flat fee + performance once proven

Discovery projects exist to generate a quick win, earn trust, and set up the larger second project — price them to close, not to maximize.

## Universal Funnel Rules

- Advertising must be measurable: every campaign gets one clear success metric before launch
- **Two-step lead generation**: step 1, cheap easy-consumption ad to identify interested people; step 2, advertise the offer to the interactors
- The irresistible offer: ease them in with low commitment + high value
- Once a funnel wins: raise volume, then add the next funnel — never both changes at once
